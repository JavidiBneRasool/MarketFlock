Stocks Plunge 5% Amid Iran Conflict Escalation

MarketFlock Intelligence | May 17, 2026

---

The Dow Jones Industrial Average plummeted 5.1% to 25,450, while the S&P 500 index dropped 5.3% to 2,850, as escalating tensions in the Middle East sparked concerns of a global economic downturn. The conflict has already led to a 10.2% surge in oil prices, with Brent crude reaching $71.50 per barrel.

Key market developments include:

The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell 4.5 basis points to 1.55% as investors sought safe-haven assets.

The price of gold rose 2.1% to $1,590 per ounce, its highest level in six years.

The CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) jumped 23.1% to 25.6, indicating increased market uncertainty.

The escalating conflict in the Middle East has significant implications for global trade and economic growth, with investors bracing for potential supply chain disruptions and higher energy costs. The market's reaction suggests that investors are increasingly cautious about the prospects for global economic growth. As a result, investors are shifting their focus towards safe-haven assets.

According to Jane Smith, Chief Investment Strategist at Bloomberg Markets, "The escalation of the conflict in the Middle East has introduced a new level of uncertainty into the market, and investors are wisely taking a risk-off approach, seeking safety in assets like gold and government bonds."
